Enterprise Connectivity Challenges in South-central Alaska

Anchorage is Alaska's economic center, home to Joint Base Elmendorf-Richardson, ConocoPhillips upstream operations, Providence Alaska Medical Center, and the state's primary aviation and logistics hub. But the region operates in near-total isolation from terrestrial fiber infrastructure. The North Slope oilfields are hundreds of miles from Anchorage, reachable only by air and limited roads. Fishing fleet operations and seafood processing plants depend on radio and satellite — often unreliable 1990s-era systems. Remote mining operations, construction camps, and research stations across Alaska operate with zero connectivity alternatives.

Traditional satellite internet (HughesNet, Viasat) is too slow for modern operations. Fixed wireless in rural Alaska is nonexistent. And fiber? It simply doesn't exist outside Anchorage proper. Enterprise Starlink changes everything — delivering low-latency, high-bandwidth connectivity to every corner of Alaska. 2pifi is the only provider with Arctic-grade installation expertise, extreme-weather operations experience, and the systems expertise to integrate Starlink into mission-critical North Slope, aviation, and medical facility operations.

2pifi conducts a comprehensive technical assessment for every Anchorage and remote Alaska site — satellite visibility, extreme weather resilience, integration with existing VSAT or legacy systems, and Arctic operational procedures — before committing to a deployment timeline.
