Frequently Asked Questions — Orlando

Is enterprise Starlink available in Orlando and surrounding areas? ▼
Yes. Enterprise Starlink is available throughout the Orlando metro and surrounding Orange County, including Kissimmee, Sanford, Altamonte Springs, Winter Park, Ocoee, Apopka, Lake Mary, and Clermont. Unlike consumer Starlink, enterprise service includes public static IPv4 addresses, no sold-out area restrictions, and priority support. 2pifi handles the full deployment — you don't touch any of it.
How does hurricane season affect Starlink deployment in Orlando? ▼
All 2pifi deployments in Central Florida follow hurricane-rated installation standards — reinforced masts, ballasted mounting, redundant grounding, and rapid disconnect protocols. Starlink equipment itself is rated for extreme weather. 2pifi's approach includes failover redundancy so if one connection is disrupted, your network automatically falls back to secondary carriers. For mission-critical facilities, we recommend multi-carrier deployments.
What if my Orlando location is blocked by trees or buildings? ▼
We identify viability during the technical assessment — before you're committed to anything. If Starlink won't work due to obstructions, we'll recommend the best alternative: fixed wireless broadband from our network of 800+ providers, 5G, or dedicated wireline. Orlando's rapid development means we often find fixed wireless to be faster and more cost-effective than waiting for fiber buildouts.
How long does deployment take for a theme park or resort property? ▼
For single-site deployments, 2-3 weeks from assessment to operational. Large resort or multi-building properties may require 3-4 weeks to account for complex site surveys, rooftop access coordination, and integration testing across multiple systems. Multi-site rollouts are managed as coordinated projects with expedited timelines available for mission-critical facilities. We work around operational schedules and peak seasons.
Can Starlink support our healthcare system's HIPAA and uptime requirements?
▼
Yes. Enterprise Starlink is HIPAA-eligible with SLA-backed 99.9% uptime guarantees. 2pifi pairs it with dedicated encrypted transport, automatic failover to secondary carriers, and 24/7 managed NOC monitoring. AdventHealth and other major healthcare systems across Central Florida use this exact model. We also support network redundancy architectures for compliance-critical operations.
