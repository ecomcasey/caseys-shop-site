Enterprise Connectivity Challenges in San Diego County

San Diego County spans over 4,000 square miles of coastal terrain, urban sprawl, and military-restricted zones. Qualcomm, General Atomics, Northrop Grumman, and Naval Base San Diego anchor the regional economy, but they're not all downtown—they're scattered across Sorrento Valley, the aerospace corridor toward Carlsbad, and remote hillside facilities in Escondido and beyond.

Many critical San Diego facilities face real connectivity challenges. Military installations require secure, redundant connectivity for classified operations. Port terminals along the waterfront need reliable broadband for logistics coordination. Biotech campuses in outlying areas often lack adequate fiber, and new defense contractor facilities opening in less-developed zones face months of broadband buildout delays. For businesses that can't wait—or can't justify the cost of bringing fiber to remote sites—enterprise Starlink and fixed wireless provide immediate solutions.

2pifi conducts thorough technical assessments for every San Diego site—satellite visibility accounting for coastal haze and urban obstructions, terrain analysis, line-of-sight surveys for fixed wireless, and integration requirements—before committing to deployment. If a site isn't viable for Starlink, we recommend the best alternative: fixed wireless, 5G, or dedicated fiber, and we manage the entire project to completion.

A typical single-site deployment takes 2 to 3 weeks from initial assessment to verified operational. Yes. 2pifi has extensive experience deploying to defense contractors, facilities near military installations, and other sensitive operational environments.
